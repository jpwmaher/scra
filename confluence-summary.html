SCRa - Confluence summary

Introduction


NHSD are building a new Summary Care Record Application (SCRa) with the primary objective of making the service accessible to users 'on the move'.

To enable this, the following high level requirements would need to be met:

  • User can access service across range of device types (mobile, tablet, desktop etc.)
  • User can authenticate without need for Smart Card
  • User can access service without being on N3

Within this context, Hippo Digital completed a Discovery from 5th March to 31st August 2018 with the aim of achieving the following:

  • Assess the feasibility for Paramedics piloting mobile access to the New SCRa
  • Identify opportunities for improving UX in the New SCRa relative to the Legacy SCRa
  • Identify opportunities for new capabilities in the New SCRa
  • Provide requirements and designs to support delivery of the New SCRa

Requirements Backlog & Designs


The backlog at this stage captures requirements (i.e. problems) rather than solution (i.e. how to deliver change). The next stage would be to elborate on these requirements, effectively creating a delivery backlog for the highest priority requirements.

The backlog is structure into a VMOST:

  • Vision - Where NHSD sees itself in future (in this case, enabling the NHS to be paperless by 2020). Realising a vision will depend of successful completion of several missions.
  • Mission – a mission is a large aspirational target of change, typically the purpose of a business unit / service. It can be broken down into several objectives.
  • Objectives (represented by Epics in JIRA) – these are the milestones that you need to achieve in order to progress towards the mission. By capturing a problem to be solved, they will help focus people towards a common goal. They should be defined in a SMART fashion (Specific, Measurable, Actionable, Realistic and Timebound)
  • Strategies (represented by Stories in JIRA) - areas of focus which will allow you to meet your objectives.
  • Tactics - the specific changes the delivery team will make whilst pursuing a strategy. These have will be defined by the delivery team at a later date.
