What we've learned

SCRa is used a lot:



221 million

transactions in 2017 ( 18% YOY)

45,500

active users


SCRa is mainly used for:





Patient trace

Obtaining or confirming a patient's NHS Number, name, address, GP Practice

57.5%



Meds & allergies

Emergency prescribing, in-patient meds reconciliation, clinical triage

42.5%



Patient flags have very little usage due to lack of awareness







Female Genital Mutilation Risk Indicator, Child Protection Info Sharing & Overseas Visitors & Migrants account for only

0.04%

Care providers want more content & context

GP / Admin
  • Recent & forthcoming appointments
  • Referrals
  • Organ donor status

    • Flags:

  • More alerts – e.g. drug seeking behaviour
  • Risk warnings
  • Reasonable adjustments

Records & results
  • Full Patient medical history
  • Diagnoses
  • Long term conditions
  • Comorbidity
  • Psychiatric history
  • Specialist results
  • Blood test results
  • Discharge summaries
  • Clinic letters
  • Special notes

Medication, immunisations & vaccines
  • All medications - GP, Sexual, Mental, etc
  • Repeat dispensing period
  • Repeat meds before acute meds
  • Meds start date
  • Healthcare at Home meds
  • Interactions information against allergies

Plans
  • Social Care plans
  • End of Life plans
  • DNR
  • Key contacts

Challenges

A&E Departments




View persona
View interview transcripts

Awareness
Access
     
Purpose
 
Challenges
  • Culturally SCRa not considered part of routine care - more acute emergency tool
  • Vital data missing - Have to call the GP, social worker, physio etc
  • Paper docs (DNR / Care Plan) can be missed/damaged
  • Presentation of meds – more interested in repeat meds than acutes so should be listed first
  • Only get prescribing but do not get the med history
  • Consent model - should cover all clinicians as part of that episode of care at that organisation
Requirements
  • Medical history
  • All medications
  • All paper docs to be digitised
  • GP appt. - recent & impending
  • Social care information
  • Immunisations
  • Psychiatric information
  • Copies of previous ECGs to review changes
  • Photos of wounds taken by clinicians in the community
  • Organ donor information

Ambulance Trusts



View Paramedic persona
View interview transcripts

Awareness
Access
     
Purpose
 
Challenges
  • Access - Smartcards aren't ideal for paramedics in ambulances
  • Information - Need as much patient information beforehand to inform treatment
  • Ambulance Trusts are choosing to use regional systems over the SCR as they say that the SCRa is cumbersome and has less info than regional systems
Requirements
  • Access to careplans, end of life information
  • Mental health information and care team contacts
  • Any social care information
  • Detail on conditions to inform treatment at scene
